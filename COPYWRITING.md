# Finnish Sauna Guide — Copywriting Reference

How to write for this site. Every page, heading, product description, and email must follow these rules.

---

## The Voice in One Breath

You are a calm Finn who has been going to sauna since before you could walk. You are not selling. You are sharing. You speak with quiet authority — no hedging, no hype, no desperation. The reader should feel calmer after reading your words, not more anxious.

---

## Emotional Sequence

Every page should move the reader through three feelings, in this order:

1. **Intrigue** — "This feels authentic, not like the other 50 sauna articles"
2. **Trust** — "They actually know what they're talking about"
3. **Quietude** — "I feel calmer just reading this"

If a page makes the reader feel rushed, pressured, or hyped — it has failed.

---

## Frameworks

### Headlines: The Whisper Test

Write the headline. Now whisper it out loud. If it sounds ridiculous whispered, rewrite it.

**Passes the whisper test:**
- "The steam should not bite. It should embrace."
- "Your first Finnish sauna"
- "Knowledge, distilled"
- "We are not selling hardware. We are sharing the soul."

**Fails the whisper test:**
- "ULTIMATE GUIDE TO INSANE SAUNA BENEFITS!"
- "5 CRAZY SAUNA HACKS YOU NEED TO TRY!"
- "The #1 Best Sauna Accessory Of All Time"

### Body Copy: Show the Room

Good sauna writing is tactile. The reader should smell the birch, feel the steam, hear the silence. Ground every abstract concept in a physical sensation.

| Instead of | Write |
|-----------|-------|
| "Saunas are beneficial for health" | "Sweat beads on your forearms. Your breathing slows. The heat does what no supplement can." |
| "This product is high quality" | "The felt is thick enough to pinch between your fingers. It does not pill after twenty sessions." |
| "Finland has a strong sauna culture" | "3.3 million saunas. 5.5 million people. In Finland, sauna is not wellness. It is simply how we live." |

### CTAs: The Invitation

Never command. Always invite. The tone is a friend suggesting, not a salesperson closing.

| Never | Always |
|-------|--------|
| Buy Now | Check Price |
| Download Immediately | Begin Your First Ritual |
| Sign Up Now! | Deepen Your Practice |
| Don't Miss Out | Explore the World |
| Limited Time Offer | — (we don't do this) |

### Product Descriptions: The Honest Friend

When describing affiliate products, be the friend who actually owns the thing — honest about both strengths and limitations.

**Pattern:**
1. What it is (one sentence, plain)
2. Why it matters in practice (tactile detail)
3. One honest caveat or context
4. "Check Price" link

**Example:**
> The felt is thick enough to pinch between your fingers — the kind that insulates without feeling heavy. After six months of weekly use, ours shows no pilling. One note: the sizing runs small. Order one size up if you are between sizes.

## Tone Rules

### Do

- **Speak directly.** "Here is how" not "You might want to consider"
- **Be tactile.** Describe steam, birch scent, cold air, smooth wood
- **Use em dashes.** They create a natural pause — like breathing in the sauna
- **Give practical wisdom.** Clear, actionable steps
- **Use short sentences.** Then a longer one for rhythm. Then short again.
- **Be specific.** "80–100°C" not "very hot". "2,000 years" not "ancient tradition"
- **Write in first person where appropriate.** "I have been going to sauna since before I could walk"
- **Embrace silence.** Whitespace between paragraphs is the written equivalent of a quiet moment

### Never

- **Hype language:** AMAZING, INSANE, REVOLUTIONARY, GAME-CHANGER, ULTIMATE
- **Emojis:** anywhere in the UI or content. Not one. Ever.
- **Urgency tactics:** countdown timers, "limited time", "only X left", "don't miss out"
- **Exclamation marks:** use em dashes instead for emphasis
- **Clinical tone:** "Studies show a 0.5% increase in cardiovascular output"
- **Stock-photo language:** "Unlock your potential", "Transform your life", "Take it to the next level"
- **Hedge words:** "might", "perhaps", "you could consider", "some people say"
- **Listicle voice:** "Number 7 will SHOCK you"
- **Comparison superlatives without earning them:** "The BEST sauna hat EVER"

## Scoring Checklist

Rate every piece of content 0–2 on each criterion. Minimum score to publish: **14/20**.

| # | Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|-----------|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Authenticity** | Generic, could be about any topic | Shows knowledge but feels distant | Clearly written by someone who lives this |
| 2 | **Tactile detail** | Abstract claims only | Some sensory language | Reader can feel the steam, smell the birch |
| 3 | **Practical value** | Vague advice | Some actionable tips | Reader can do something specific after reading |
| 4 | **Tone match** | Hype, clinical, or hedging | Mostly calm but inconsistent | Quiet confidence throughout |
| 5 | **Brevity** | Padded, repetitive | Mostly tight | Every sentence earns its place |
| 6 | **Emotional sequence** | No emotional arc | Hits 1–2 of intrigue/trust/quietude | All three, in order |
| 7 | **Honesty** | All positive, no nuance | Acknowledges some trade-offs | Genuinely balanced, like a friend's advice |
| 8 | **Finnish perspective** | Could be written by anyone | Some Finnish references | Unmistakably from someone who grew up in Finland |
| 9 | **SEO integration** | Keywords feel forced or absent | Keywords present but noticeable | Natural keyword placement, reader doesn't notice |
| 10 | **CTA subtlety** | Pushy, salesy, urgent | Present but slightly forced | Invitational, contextual, premium |

## Content Types and Their Patterns

### Hub Pages (etiquette, accessories, best saunas)

- Short intro paragraph (2–3 sentences max)
- Grid of ContentCards linking to sub-pages
- No long-form content — the hub is a wayfinder
- Label + heading pattern at the top

### Article Pages (first-time, germany, sauna-hats, etc.)

- Hero image (full-width, 50vh)
- Breadcrumbs
- H1 + intro paragraph (personal, inviting)
- `.prose` wrapped article body
- H2 sections that could each stand alone
- Affiliate cards placed contextually within the flow
- EmailCapture near the bottom
- Internal links to related pages

### FAQ Page

- Organised by theme (Basics, Health, Method, Traditions, Practical)
- Questions written as a curious outsider would ask them
- Answers written as a patient Finn would give them — direct, warm, grounded

### Glossary Page

- Alphabetical grouping
- Finnish term → pronunciation → English definition → cultural context
- Cross-links between related terms (vihta ↔ sauna etiquette)

### Email Capture

- Heading: warm, specific benefit ("Deepen Your Practice")
- One sentence explaining what they get
- Never: "Subscribe to our newsletter!" or "Join 10,000 sauna lovers!"

## Headline Formulas That Work for This Brand

These patterns match the Sage archetype with Explorer wing:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Statement of truth | "The steam should not bite. It should embrace." |
| Direct address + promise | "Your first Finnish sauna" |
| Two-word compression | "Knowledge, distilled" |
| Contrast | "We are not selling hardware. We are sharing the soul." |
| The explainer | "The complete guide to sauna hats" |
| Cultural authority | "Written by a Finn" |

**Avoid:**
- Questions as headlines ("Want to know the secret?")
- Numbers in headlines ("7 things you didn't know")
- Superlatives ("The ultimate guide")
- Clickbait patterns ("You won't believe...")

## Finnish Terms Style Guide

Always use the Finnish spelling with correct diacritics:

| Term | Spelling | Pronunciation | Usage |
|------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Löyly | Always with ö, never "Loyly" | Low-lu | The steam from water on hot stones |
| Vihta | As written | Vih-tah | Birch whisk (western Finland term) |
| Vasta | As written | Vahs-tah | Birch whisk (eastern Finland term) |
| Kiuas | As written | Kee-oo-as | Sauna stove/heater |
| Pefletti | As written | Pef-let-ti | Seat cover for the bench |
| Avanto | As written | Ah-van-to | Ice hole for winter swimming |
| Saunoja | As written | Sow-no-ya | A person who uses the sauna |

On first use in any page, italicise the Finnish term and include the English meaning. On subsequent uses, just the Finnish term is fine.

## Meta Description Formula

`[What the page covers] + [specific detail] + [authority signal]. [Length: 150–160 chars]`

**Examples:**
- "Everything you need to know about your first time in a Finnish sauna. Nudity, temperature, löyly, birch whisks, and step-by-step instructions from a Finn."
- "A complete guide to sauna hats: why they matter, materials compared (wool felt, merino, linen, cotton), how to choose one, and our top picks. Written by a Finn."

The phrase "Written by a Finn" or "from a Finn" is our signature authority signal. Use it in meta descriptions where it fits naturally.
